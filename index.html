Reza Rahimi

Ph.D.
Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering
University of Virginia

I received my PhD in the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at the University of Virginia, and I am a researcher at the Center for Research on Intelligent Storage and Processing in Memory (CRISP), advised by Professor Kevin Skadron. My research is at the intersection of computer architecture along with algorithm, compiler, and machine learning, and is focused on developing specialized, near-data hardware accelerators for big data applications. My research has resulted in several publications at top-tier venues (such as MICRO, ASPLOS, HPCA, FCCM, and ICRA). My work has received the “Best of CAL” award in 2019, and nominations for the best paper award in HPCA 2020 and FCCM 2020. I received my Master’s degrees in Computer Engineering from the Sharif University and Bachelor’s degree in Computer Engineering from Iran University of Science & Technology.
